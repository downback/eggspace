A Haven for Phantoms
22.04.22-06.05.22
Peilian Li and Hanna Samoson
Text by Rachel TonThat
In the final exhibition of Egg’s Intervening Spheres, A Haven for Phantoms, the last artist of the cycle joins with the first to present two stories of cybernetic entities created by human idiosyncrasies and failures. ECAL’s Peilian Li exhibits work that references wordplay created by Chinese censorship. An homage to the alpaca, which in Mandarin is a near homonym for “fuck you mother,” is a playful query into the efficacy of suppression, when now even the emoji of an alpaca carries double meaning. Interacting with her golems more directly, Estonian artist Hanna Samoson shares a digital book she created which documents an attempt to purchase a computer to better create 3D models, relying on those same 3D models to help her raise the money. Alive but nonliving, they challenge her competence and are expressed as antagonists within the story.

Peilian Li is a Swiss-Chinese visual artist, performer and writer. Her works forgo ordinary logic in favor of an onirical elation of ideas that often touch the identity issues of being an Asian immigrant in the Western world. She graduated with a Master in visual arts at ECAL / Ecole Cantonale d’Art de Lausanne in 2022 and she currently lives and works in Lausanne, Switzerland.

Hanna Samoson (1987) explores the boundaries of art and tries to perceive the unknown. Being in constant motion as an intuitive creator, her work is characterized by quick and spontaneous decisions. She studied photography at the Estonian Academy of Arts and graduated from the FAMU Academy of Performing Arts in Prague this year. In the autumn of 2020 she walked the future route of Rail Baltic from Ikla to Tallinn as an endurance art performance.
